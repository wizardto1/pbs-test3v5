Performance-Based Balance Scale

Prior to testing, please refer to the full instructions for settings, space requirements, time and equipment needed (including a gait belt for guarding), instructions and scoring schema.

Description: 20-item scale with dichotomous (yes/no) scores designed to measure the balance ability of community-living older adults

Settings: Out-patient clinics, hospitals, rehab centers, independent living centers, and possibly homes

Space requirements:

  1. An uncluttered straight walkway approximately 22-24 ft (7 m) in length
  2. Uncluttered open space approximately 5 ft × 5 ft (1.5 m × 1.5 m)

Time: Excluding set up time, 5 to 15 minutes depending on client’s performance ability

Equipment needed:

  1. Stopwatch or the PBS app
  2. Tempur® foam (about 2 ft × 2 ft [60 cm × 60 cm] block of 4 in (10 cm) high, medium-density)*
  3. Stool or step (8 in [20 cm] high)
  4. Tape measure (20 ft [6 m]) or pieces of string (one 10 ft [3 m] long and one 24 in [60 cm] long)
  5. Duct- or masking-type tape (preferably colored tape to provide contrast to floor surface)
  6. Standard firm chair with back and armrests (about 17-18 in [43-46 cm] from floor to seat)
  7. Two (2) stacked shoe boxes (12 in [30 cm] long, 7.5 in [19 cm] wide & 9.5 in [24 cm] high)
  8. Two (2) minimum 6 ft [2 m] folding wood rules or self-locking tape measures, or four (4) standard, single point straight canes (about 35 in [90 cm] long)
  9. Gait belt for guarding the client

Instructions for examiner:

  1. Read the “tester” information for each item before testing.
  2. Read the “client” instructions to the client.
  3. Demonstrate the tasks (test item), if instructed.
  4. Allow a practice trial, if instructed.
  5. Ask the client to perform each task independently, but with guarding.
  6. Allow the client a second trial, if needed; score the best trial.
  7. Time tasks and record time, if indicated; circle foot / leg where indicated.
  8. Score each task immediately after completion.
  9. Stop the test when the client scores a 0 on 3 consecutive items.

Scoring:**

1 = Able to perform ALL specified conditions of the task independently (without assistance) and safely

0 = Unable to complete the task, unable to perform the task without assistance, or unable to perform the task with the standard requirements

Score unable (0) if:

  • client is unwilling to perform an item.
  • tester chooses not to ask client to perform an item for safety reasons.
Total scores range from 0 (worst performance) to 20 (best performance).
